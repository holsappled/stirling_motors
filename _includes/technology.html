Our Technology

Robert Stirling patented the first hot air engine with economizer (regenerator) in the early 19th century. Over time IC engines, and electric motors have displaced nearly all Stirling engine applications. However, due to its high efficiency and other attributes, the Stirling is making a comeback.

Our technology is based on our implementation of a gamma-configuration stirling engine. (US patent application #13/949795)

Our first product will be a 3HP engine for residential and commercial applications. Our second release will be a 1.5HP model for small residential and mobile applications. The engines will run on natural gas, propane, or gasoline. Future plans include 5 and 10 HP models for larger residential and commercial applications, and mil-spec versions.
